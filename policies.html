Privacy Policy for Orbiteer

Last updated: 7/3-2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Orbiteer ("the App"), developed by PDA ("we," "us," "our"), collects, uses, and shares information when you use the App. If you have questions about this policy, contact us at immerse.pda@gmail.com.

Information We Collect

Automatically, when you first open the App

The App creates an anonymous identity for you using Firebase Authentication as soon as you first open it. This happens silently and does not involve any personal information — no name, email, or phone number is collected at this stage. This identity is a randomly generated identifier used solely to let your progress, friend code, and leaderboard entry work correctly.

If you choose to sign in with Google

Signing in with Google is entirely optional. If you choose to do so, we use it to link your existing anonymous identity to your Google account, so your progress can be restored if you reinstall the App or switch devices. This uses Google's standard sign-in credential to verify your identity; we do not separately collect or store your Google profile name, photo, or email address in our own systems beyond what Firebase Authentication requires to complete the sign-in.

If you set a display name for the leaderboard

If you choose to participate in the leaderboard feature, you'll set a display name. This display name, together with your game score, your best achieved tile, your current rank and prestige level, your selected theme, and a randomly generated 6-character friend code, are stored and may be visible to other players — this is the leaderboard's intended purpose. Display names are checked against a moderation filter before being accepted.

Locally on your device

Your gameplay progress — XP, badges, prestige level, unlocked themes, settings preferences, and score history — is stored locally on your device using local storage (Room database). This local data is not transmitted to us except for the specific fields described above that power the optional leaderboard feature.

Advertising

The App uses Google AdMob to display ads, including interstitial ads (shown between games) and rewarded ads (optional, in exchange for an in-game bonus). AdMob may collect and use device identifiers (such as an advertising ID) and other technical information to serve and measure ads, and may use this information to personalize the ads you see, consistent with Google's own data practices. You can review Google's practices at policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Crash and diagnostic data

The App uses Firebase Crashlytics to help us identify and fix crashes and errors. This may include technical information such as device model, operating system version, and the circumstances of a crash, but does not include your gameplay data or leaderboard information.

How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

  • Let the App function (save your progress, generate a friend code, keep your identity consistent across sessions)
  • Operate the leaderboard and friends features
  • Diagnose and fix technical problems
  • Display and measure advertising

We do not sell your information to third parties.

Third-Party Services

The App relies on the following third-party services, each of which processes data according to its own privacy policy:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Crashlytics) — firebase.google.com/support/privacy
  • Google AdMob — policies.google.com/technologies/ads

Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account remains active. If you delete your account (see below), your associated leaderboard data and local progress are removed. We may retain limited technical data where required for security, fraud prevention, or legal compliance.

Your Rights and Choices

Deleting your account and data

You can delete your account and all associated data at any time from within the App: Settings → Danger Zone → Delete Account & Reset All Progress. This removes your local progress, your leaderboard entry, your reserved display name, and your underlying account identity.

If you're unable to access the App, you can also request deletion by emailing immerse.pda@gmail.com with your friend code if you have it, or a description of the device/timeframe you used the App.

Your rights under GDPR (if you're in the EU/EEA)

You have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data, and to object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights, use the in-app deletion option above or contact us at the email listed at the top of this policy.

Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the relevant age of digital consent in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can address it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or your data, contact us at: immerse.pda@gmail.com
